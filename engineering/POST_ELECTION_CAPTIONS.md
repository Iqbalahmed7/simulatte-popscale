## LONG (~270 words, Substack/blog)

> We staked TMC. Bengal voted otherwise.
>
> The Construct — Simulatte's election prediction system — called TMC at 194 ± 10 seats with majority. The final result: BJP 203 / TMC 84 / Left+Congress 6 / Others 1. A clean directional miss outside our stated confidence interval on every party.
>
> **What we weighted:**
> – TMC organisational depth in panchayats (the 2021 machine)
> – Muslim consolidation around TMC in Murshidabad (90% TMC baseline)
> – CAA backlash in the Matua belt
> – Jungle Mahal tribal anti-BJP sentiment from 2021
> – 2021 baseline anchoring (213 TMC seats)
>
> **What actually decided it:**
> – Hindu consolidation as a statewide frame, not a localized factor (BJP voteshare 45.1% vs ~22% modelled)
> – SIR voter-roll deletions producing structural turnout asymmetry
> – 4-way Muslim vote fragmentation (TMC / AIMIM / AJUP / INDI) yielding BJP pluralities, not consolidation
> – Reverse-direction Matua swing — identification with BJP's CAA delivery
> – BJP organisational expansion in rural Bengal post-2021
> – And the one that stings most: the depth of anti-incumbency that wasn't visible in any survey, news cycle, or panchayat dataset we fed the model. Simulations are good at the visible. The unseen is where they lose.
>
> For the record: not political, no horse in this race. Would have preferred to be right.
>
> Each miss makes The Construct sharper. The calibration loop processes the per-constituency results tomorrow morning. Priors update mechanically. India is the hardest country in the world to model — twenty-eight states, every election its own physics. We'll be back for the next nail-biter.
